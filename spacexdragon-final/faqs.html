Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to Common Inquiries About SpaceX Dragon

Could SpaceX send a Dragon to the Moon?

The Dragon could be sent to lunar orbit but lacks the capability to land due to insufficient delta-v. The Falcon Heavy can send Dragon 2 to the moon, but landing requires much more propellant than Dragon carries.

What is Red Dragon?

Red Dragon was a proposed mission to send a modified Dragon capsule to Mars using a Falcon Heavy rocket. It was intended as a scientific lander but has not been funded or officially announced.

Will the Dragon be used for a manned mission to Mars?

No. Dragon is too small and lacks the required life support for the long duration of a Mars mission.

Why does the Dragon "berth" to the ISS instead of dock?

Dragon uses a Common Berthing Mechanism (CBM), which requires astronauts to manually connect the spacecraft using the Canadarm. Docking systems, like those used on other spacecraft, are faster and autonomous.

Can Dragon 1 be used to evacuate the International Space Station?

No. Dragon 1 is not equipped with seating or rapid launch capabilities needed for emergency evacuations, unlike Soyuz spacecraft.

Can Dragon or Dragon 2 reboost the International Space Station?

No. Dragon does not have the fuel, thrusters, or proper ports to reboost the ISS effectively.

How much Delta V does the Dragon 2 have?

Dragon 2 has approximately 420 m/s of delta-v, provided by its SuperDraco thrusters and 3500 pounds of propellant.

What would happen if one of Dragon 2's SuperDraco thrusters failed during landing?

Dragon 2 can handle the failure of up to two SuperDracos. If multiple failures occur, parachutes are deployed for a safe splashdown.

Will SuperDraco replace Draco?

No. Draco engines are used for in-orbit adjustments, while SuperDraco engines are reserved for launch escape and landing scenarios.

What is PICA-X? Does it ablate?

PICA-X is a heat shield material designed for reentry. It protects Dragon spacecraft by ablating during atmospheric entry.

Could Dragon 2 service the Hubble Space Telescope?

Technically, it could. However, building a new telescope is more cost-effective than modifying Dragon 2 for such a mission.

Why does Dragon's trunk stay attached during an abort scenario?

The trunk stabilizes the capsule during high-speed aborts, ensuring it remains oriented correctly. It is jettisoned before reentry.

How does a Dragon reenter the atmosphere and splashdown?

Reentry involves a deorbit burn, protection from heat with a PICA-X shield, and a controlled descent using parachutes for splashdown.
